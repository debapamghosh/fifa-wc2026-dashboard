Canada's previous tournament appearances in 1986 and 2022 both ended in three consecutive group-stage defeats. LOS ANGELES вҖ” The weight of a nation settles onto the SoFi Stadium turf the moment the anthem fades and the first whistle cuts through 70,000 voices.

When the United States opens its home World Cup against Paraguay, the numbers tell a story of overwhelming structural advantage. This is a co-host playing inside a 70,000-seat fortress, on a pristine enclosed pitch, against a side that hasn't tasted World Cup football in sixteen years. Pochettino's squad has spent the entire buildup in familiar conditions вҖ” sleeping in American beds, training on American grass, absorbing the adulation of a country that has waited 32 years to host this tournament again.

Paraguay, by contrast, arrives carrying the invisible weight of South American qualifying's relentless grind. Gustavo Alfaro's side conceded just 10 goals across 18 CONMEBOL qualifiers вҖ” a defensive record that commands respect вҖ” but the transition from AsunciГіn to Los Angeles creates a subtle dislocation that analytics struggle to fully capture.

Paraguay's response will be predictable but effective: a compact, suffocating 4-4-2 block designed to choke central spaces and frustrate the crowd into anxiety.

The danger for the USA is psychological, not tactical. Host nations carry a brutal expectation premium вҖ” the crowd that lifts you at 0-0 can crush you at 0-1. Paraguay knows this. Alfaro will set his side up to survive the opening 30-minute storm, then unleash AlmirГіn on tired legs via precisely the sort of counter-attack that suffocated better teams throughout qualifying.

But SoFi Stadium's enclosed roof amplifies crowd noise to a degree few visiting teams can psychologically withstand for 90 minutes. The second half, when Paraguay's concentration historically dips and the USA's superior depth begins to tell, is where the game will be decided.

The hosts break through. Probably not comfortably вҖ” but they break through.
